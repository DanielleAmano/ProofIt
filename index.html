Proof It!

Have you ever looked at an article, a website or a cook book and something didn’t look right?

Space, comma, dot, apostrophes, hyphen, spelling check, they all assemble the perfect view. The way you look at a page and just smile, because it’s so pretty and organized.

Let’s take it from the start, I’ll be focusing on proofing a cook book. Before we get to work, we need to understand the client’s expectations and needs so we can do the job in the best way possible.

The Steps We’ll Take

  • Don’t forget the cover page, where it all begins, all the way down to the fine print.
  • Table of contents – go over very thoroughly, even if it’s long. Sometimes you’ll have to compare to the recipe page itself. For example, if there is a spelling problem, I’ll check that it’s not in the headline as well.
  • And then came the recipes. The optimal thing is to see all that needs to be corrected while going through the recipe all at once. If it’s not the case, please take your time and check spaces, commas etc. at your own pace. Make sure the shortening of the measurements is correct with the apostrophes in the right place. Also, space after a comma, dot and hyphen. Moreover, we need to check that there is no extra space between two words and unnecessary one at the beginning of a paragraph.
  • Personally, I’m looking at unity and anything that looks strange and commenting on it, although the client has the final say on what changes he actually wants.
  • I’m also looking at the page structure regarding the unity look.

    • Bonus: While you’re checking the book, you can start tasting those tasty recipes.
